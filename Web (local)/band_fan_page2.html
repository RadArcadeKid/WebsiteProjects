Maps of Daft Punk's Tour

These are maps from Daft Punk's Alive 2007, which lasted from April 2006 until December, 2007. The tour was exclusive to Daft Punk, and the group played remixes of their older and (at the time) newer work from their first few studio albums. In almost all venues, they played inside a giant pyramid with LED's around it, similar to what many modern DJ's like DeadMau5 currently use. This was one of their most inconic tours, and quite possibly their last. This is due to both their age as a group and lack of new albums.

More Info

Daft Punk, as a group, are just as prolific and exciting as they are mysterious and facination. They've played around the world on their live shows, but unfortunately, it looks like they don't plan on putting out many more tours. Given that their last one was over a decade ago, and that both members are 46, it doesn't seem like they plan on doing a massive tour anytime soon.
Surprisngly, though, they did do a recent colaboration with an artist known as THE WEEKND, and helped to make the beats for the background of his song, I Feel It Coming. This was their first release in over six years, and even though it wasn't a studio album, it perhaps could be the start of something greater. Maybe even another tour?

Jake Henson - ATLS2200
